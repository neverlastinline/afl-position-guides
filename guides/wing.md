# Wing — AFL Masters Development Guide

## Role Summary

The wing is the engine room of the team, running up and down the boundary side of the ground to link defence and attack. As a winger you cover more ground than almost any other position, so your job is to provide a constant outlet for teammates moving the ball forward and to drift back and help defensively when the ball goes the other way. For an over-35 player, the wing rewards smart running and reading the play over flat-out pace.

## Positioning & Movement

- Hold the corridor between the centre square and the boundary; don't drift too far inside or too wide.
- Run in straight lines up and down your wing rather than across the ground, so you arrive with options ahead.
- Push forward in attack and create space ahead of the carrier; work back to give defenders a spare on turnover.
- Communicate with your centre and half-back so you're never both leaving the same gap open.

## Core Skills & Drills

### 1. Running and Receiving on the Move
Most touches come at pace, so take a handball or chest mark while moving.
- Partner feeds handballs as you jog past cones, then kick to a target 30m away.

### 2. Accurate Medium-Range Kicking
A reliable 30-40m kick into the forward 50 is your most valuable weapon.
- Kick at a goalpost from 35m, ten reps off each foot, same spot.

### 3. Two-Sided Handball
Quick contests demand a clean handball under pressure to either side.
- Handball against a wall or partner, alternating hands, rhythm before speed.

### 4. Defensive Tracking
Following your opponent back and applying a tackle or smother stops easy rebounds.
- Shadow a partner running random angles for 20m, staying goal-side.

## Common Mistakes

- Drifting too far inside and crowding the midfield, leaving your wing open for a switch.
- Ball-watching and forgetting your defensive run.
- Sprinting flat-out early and having nothing left in the last quarter.
- Kicking blindly into the corridor under pressure instead of using the boundary.

## Smart Conditioning (Over-35s)

- Build aerobic base with two easy 25-30 minute runs or brisk walk-jogs a week.
- Warm up calves, hamstrings, and groin with dynamic movements before running.
- Light strength twice a week (squats, lunges, calf raises) to support the running load.
- Rotate off the wing for a quarter if gassed; fatigue is when most injuries happen.

## What Good Looks Like

- You're a reliable outlet every time your team rebounds.
- You rarely get caught out of position, helping defensively and pushing forward.
- Your disposals are clean and used, hitting targets by foot.
