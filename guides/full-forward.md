# Full Forward (FF) — AFL Masters Development Guide

## Role Summary

The Full Forward is the team's main goal-scorer, stationed deepest in attack inside the forward 50. Your job is to lead out at the ball, take strong marks, and convert shots on goal, while also occupying and pinning down the opposition's key defender. As an over-35 amateur, you do not need to be the fastest player on the ground; smart positioning, reliable hands, and accurate kicking matter far more than raw pace.

## Positioning & Movement

- Start near the goal square, then lead out toward the ball carrier to give them a clear target.
- Time your lead so you meet the ball at full stride rather than waiting flat-footed.
- Use the "lead and circle back" pattern: if the first lead is covered, peel back toward goal for a second option.
- Keep your defender on your back or hip when the ball comes in deep, so you are between them and the ball.

## Core Skills & Drills

### 1. Leading and Marking
The core of the role; if you can present and mark, you stay dangerous.
- Partner kicks to you as you lead out from a cone, 10 leads each side.
- Chest and overhead marks 15-20m from a feeder.

### 2. Set-Shot Goalkicking
Most amateur scores come from set shots, so a repeatable routine wins games.
- 10 shots from directly in front at 30m, then 10 from a slight angle, same pre-shot routine.

### 3. Body Positioning in a Contest
Holding your ground lets you mark or bring the ball to ground.
- Partner pushes lightly behind you; stand tall and present hands to a high ball.

### 4. Ground-Ball Gathering
Many forward-50 chances come off spilled marks.
- Roll or kick balls low along the ground; scoop cleanly at walking then jogging pace.

## Common Mistakes

- Leading too early or too late, so you arrive before the kick or get there flat-footed.
- Always leading to the same side, making you easy to defend.
- Rushing the set shot instead of using a steady, repeatable routine.
- Drifting too far up the ground and leaving the goal square empty when the ball comes in fast.

## Smart Conditioning (Over-35s)

- Warm up with 5-10 minutes of jogging and dynamic leg swings; cold leads are how hamstrings tear.
- Build leading repeats gradually rather than going flat-out from the first drill.
- One short session a week of calf, hamstring, and glute strength to protect what you load when leading and jumping.
- Land and contest with soft, bent knees; you can be effective without leaping at full stretch every time.

## What Good Looks Like

- You present a clear, well-timed lead and mark the ball cleanly more often than not.
- Your set shots follow the same routine and you convert a solid share from in front and slight angles.
- You stay deep and dangerous, scoring or creating chances even on a quiet day by being in the right spot.
