# Full Back (FB) — AFL Masters Development Guide

## Role Summary

The Full Back is the last line of defence, stationed in the defensive goal square and tasked with shutting down the opposition's main key forward. Your job is to spoil, intercept, and clear the ball out of the danger zone rather than win highlight-reel marks. Positioning and decision-making matter far more than raw pace, which makes it a smart role for an experienced over-35 player. Stay calm, talk constantly, and never let your direct opponent get goal-side of you.

## Positioning & Movement

- Hold a position on the goal side and slightly in front of your opponent so you attack the ball, not chase it.
- Defend the space, not just the man: drop toward the corridor and the top of the square when the ball is far.
- Watch the kicker's eyes and hips, not your opponent, to read where the ball is going.
- Always know where the goals are behind you so a hurried clearance never turns into an own-goal.

## Core Skills & Drills

### 1. Spoiling (One-Fisted Punch)
A safe spoil beats a risky mark when protecting the goals.
- Partner lobs high balls; punch with one extended fist through the line; progress to spoiling over an opponent.

### 2. Intercept Marking
Reading and taking the ball in front turns defence into attack instantly.
- Feeder kicks balls of varying height into a zone; attack each at its highest point with two hands.

### 3. Kicking Out from Full Back
Clean, accurate kick-ins relieve pressure and start counter-attacks.
- Kick-ins to targets at 20, 30, and 40m; practise a quick short pass and a long kick down the line.

### 4. Body Positioning and Front-and-Square
Using your body legally to block wins contests without outjumping anyone.
- One-on-one ground-ball contests; get body-side first, then gather at the feet.

## Common Mistakes

- Going for a spectacular mark when a simple spoil was safer deep in defence.
- Ball-watching and letting your opponent sneak to the front for an easy lead.
- Holding too tight to your man and forgetting the dangerous space in front of goal.
- Panicking on kick-ins and bombing aimlessly instead of taking the short option.

## Smart Conditioning (Over-35s)

- Thorough dynamic warm-up (leg swings, lunges, build-up runs) before contest work.
- Build short-sprint and change-of-direction tolerance gradually; back-line work is 5-15m efforts.
- Twice-weekly calf, hamstring, and glute strength to cushion landings.
- Recover fully between contests and skip jarring jump-landing drills if knees or back are sore.

## What Good Looks Like

- Your opponent finishes with few marks and goals because you spoil, body, or beat them to the front.
- You take regular intercept marks and turn defence into controlled clearances.
- You organise the back line with constant talk so the dangerous space is always covered.
