# Half Forward Flank (HFF) — AFL Masters Development Guide

## Role Summary

The half forward flank sits on the wing-side edge of the forward 50, acting as the link between the midfield and the deep forwards. Your job is to create space, win the ball on the lead or at ground level, and either snap a goal or feed it to a teammate in a better position. It is a high-running role that rewards smart movement over raw pace, which suits an over-35 player who reads the play well.

## Positioning & Movement

- Start wide and high near the boundary, then time leads back toward goal so you meet the ball at pace.
- Push up to the wing when defending to give midfielders an outlet, then work hard to get back inside 50.
- Use the boundary as your friend: leading to space near the line reduces the angles a defender can cover.
- After a contest, don't ball-watch. Crumb at the feet of taller forwards and be ready for the spill.

## Core Skills & Drills

### 1. Leading Patterns
Getting separation from your opponent is how you become an option.
- Two cones 15m apart: jog to one, explode back to the other (lead and double-back).
- Lead hard to a kicker on a 30m mark.

### 2. Marking on the Lead
Reliable chest and overhead marks turn good leads into scores.
- Partner kicks 20 lead-up balls; call for it and present two hands.

### 3. Goalkicking from an Angle
HFFs often shoot from tight angles, so a repeatable routine matters.
- 10 set shots each from 30m on a 45-degree angle, same run-up every time.

### 4. Ground-Ball and Snap
Much of your scoring is quick crumbs.
- Roll a ball, gather at pace and snap on the run; 10 clean pickups first.

## Common Mistakes

- Leading too early or standing still, so the ball-carrier has no live target.
- Drifting to the same spot as the other forwards and crowding the contest.
- Forcing low-percentage shots from impossible angles when a handball scores easier.
- Switching off defensively and not chasing or tackling on the turnover.

## Smart Conditioning (Over-35s)

- Warm up with 5-10 minutes easy jogging plus leg swings and calf raises.
- Train repeat-effort with short intervals (6 x 40m with full recovery) rather than long sprints.
- One strength session a week focused on glutes, hamstrings and calves.
- Prioritise sleep and light mobility; never push through hamstring or groin tightness.

## What Good Looks Like

- You consistently present as a clean option, winning the ball on the lead or at ground level.
- You either score or set up a teammate from inside 50 without forcing the play.
- You keep working both ways, applying pressure and helping the midfield.
