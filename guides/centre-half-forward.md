# Centre Half-Forward (CHF) — AFL Masters Development Guide

## Role Summary

The centre half-forward is the key marking target and focal point of the forward line, usually the tallest and most athletic forward, responsible for taking contested grabs, bringing the ball to ground for the smaller forwards, and kicking goals from the arc. They set up in the middle of the forward 50, ahead of the centre square, where most forward entries are funnelled. In attack they're the main aerial option for teammates kicking inside 50 and create space for crumbers; in defence they become an extra tall who can intercept opposition rebounds and slow the other team's exit.

## Positioning & Movement

- At centre bounces and stoppages, set up in front of your opponent on the defensive side, ready to lead at the ball or push back if the opposition wins clearance.
- In open play, lead hard up the ground toward the ball carrier to present a target, then work back into space or to the goal side once the kick is up.
- Pick up the opposition centre half-back; run with them when the ball goes the other way so you become a loose intercept-marking defender.
- Use lead-and-recoil patterns: time your lead to meet the kick, and if it spills, drop back into the hole as a contest or second-up option.

## Core Skills & Drills

### 1. Contested / Overhead Marking
The heart of the role; you're often outnumbered under a high entry.
- **Spoil-and-mark reps:** partner kicks high into a contest while a second player applies light front-and-square pressure; attack the ball at its highest point with both hands (8-10 reps each side).
- **Solo wall-throw marks:** throw high against a wall, mark at full stretch, land balanced.

### 2. Leading Patterns
Sharp, well-timed leads pull defenders out and give the kicker a clear target.
- **Lead-and-mark with a feeder:** from the 50, call and lead hard at an angle, mark on the move, reset, lead the other way.
- **Double-lead drill:** short fake one way, plant, explode back the other way to mark.

### 3. Kicking to a Target
You're the link between midfield and forwards, and you must convert.
- **Target-cone kicking:** partner or cones 30-40m out, kick for accuracy off a few steps, alternate feet.
- **Set-shot routine:** 35-40m at varied angles, same run-up and drop, 10-15 shots to groove it.

### 4. Ground-Ball Pickups Under Pressure
Not every entry is clean; read the spill and gather low.
- **Rolling ground-ball gather:** partner rolls the ball at you; bend at the knees, scoop with both hands, dispose in one motion.
- **Pressure pickup-and-snap:** drop in the goal square, chase with token tackling pressure, gather and snap.

## Common Mistakes

- Leading too early or too straight, so the ball ends up behind you. Time the lead to the kicker's release and angle it to present at the ball.
- Standing still in a contest. Compete and bring it to ground if you can't mark it, then crumb or pressure.
- Avoiding the contested overhead grab. Hold position, judge the drop, attack at the highest point, that's the core of the role.
- Drifting out of structure. Hold the centre corridor; wandering removes the target and crowds the small forwards.

## Smart Conditioning (Over-35s)

- Warm up for repeat leading: 5-8 min easy jog, then progressive run-throughs from 50% to near-max. Never take a full-pace lead in the first 10 minutes, cold hamstrings and calves tear on the first hard one.
- Build hamstring/calf resilience at home: Nordic-style lowers (even partial range), single-leg calf raises, Romanian deadlifts. Two short sessions a week beats stretching alone.
- Protect shoulders and landing for contested marking: band pull-aparts, external rotations, and practising landing on bent knees to spare the AC joint, ankles, and knees.
- Train the real demand: a handful of 20-40m lead-and-recover sprints with full rest, not long continuous running. Keeps soft-tissue load manageable for an over-35 body.

## What Good Looks Like

- You present at least one clean, well-timed lead per forward entry, so teammates always have a target inside 50.
- When you can't mark it, the ball hits the deck in front of the goals where your small forwards can pounce, not sailing over the pack.
- You hit the scoreboard or directly create a goal a couple of times a game, and you've held the corridor without clogging your teammates' space.
