# Forward Pocket — AFL Masters Development Guide

## Role Summary

The forward pocket plays in the attacking 50, tucked into the corner near goal and behind the key forwards. Your job is to crash packs, pounce on loose balls that spill from contests, apply defensive pressure on opposition defenders, and snap quick goals from tight angles. It is a high-effort, opportunistic role that rewards anticipation and tackling more than height or marking, which makes it a forgiving entry point for an over-35 newcomer.

## Positioning & Movement

- Hold a position 10-20m from goal, in the corner, slightly behind the key forward so you can read where the ball spills.
- Read the flight early: when a long ball comes in, drift to the likely drop zone rather than ball-watching.
- Use the goal square and boundary as reference points so you always know your angle before the ball arrives.
- Lead up at the ball or peel off to create space, but recover quickly to the danger zone once the contest resets.

## Core Skills & Drills

### 1. Ground-Ball Gathering
Most forward-pocket goals come from balls at boot level.
- Roll or kick a ball along the ground and scoop cleanly at a walk, then a jog.
- Partner feeds random ground balls left and right while you gather and steady.

### 2. Snap and Checkside Shot
You rarely get a set shot straight in front, so snapping around the body wins goals.
- From 15-20m at a tight angle, 10 snaps per side, clean low-to-high swing.

### 3. Tackling and Pressure
A forward who tackles forces turnovers and scoreboard pressure even when not scoring.
- Wrap a tackle bag or willing partner, arms pinned, feet driving.
- Shadow a "defender" and lay light two-handed tags to time your closing speed.

### 4. Leading and Presenting
A clear, sharp lead makes you a reliable target for kick-ins and entries.
- Lead hard to a marker, call for the ball, mark a chest-height feed.

## Common Mistakes

- Ball-watching and standing flat-footed instead of moving to the likely spill zone.
- Drifting too far up the ground and leaving the dangerous goal-side space unmanned.
- Rushing snaps with a wild swing instead of a controlled, balanced action.
- Switching off defensively and letting defenders rebound the ball out unopposed.

## Smart Conditioning (Over-35s)

- Warm up with 8-10 minutes of light jogging and dynamic leg swings before sprinting or kicking.
- Build calf and hamstring resilience with calf raises, single-leg balance, and Nordic-style holds once or twice a week.
- Treat snapping and twisting kicks as load: limit total shots in a session to protect groin and hip flexors.
- Prioritise recovery, stay hydrated, and stop on any sharp calf or hamstring twinge.

## What Good Looks Like

- You consistently arrive first to spilled balls and convert half-chances into goals or pressure.
- Opposition defenders never get a clean possession in your pocket because your tackling is relentless.
- You hold sensible position, read the ball's flight early, and present as a reliable target.
