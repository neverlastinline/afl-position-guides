# Half Back Flank (HBF) — AFL Masters Development Guide

## Role Summary

The Half Back Flank is a defender who sits wide of centre, halfway between the goals and the centre of the ground. Your job is two-sided: stop your opponent from getting easy possession, then launch attacks by carrying or kicking the ball out of defence. It is one of the most rewarding positions for a thinking player because reading the play matters more than raw speed. For an over-35 amateur, smart positioning and good disposal can outweigh the legs you may have lost.

## Positioning & Movement

- Hold a position slightly behind and to the side of your opponent so you see both them and the ball.
- When the ball is on the far side, drift toward the corridor to cut off cross-field kicks.
- Push up to offer a marking target when your team wins the ball, but know your path back.
- Stay goal-side of your opponent whenever the opposition is attacking.

## Core Skills & Drills

### 1. Intercept Marking
Taking a mark in front of your opponent kills attacks before they start.
- Partner kicks high from 30m; attack the ball at its highest point.

### 2. Kicking Under Light Pressure
Clean exit kicks turn defence into attack and reduce turnovers.
- Two cones 30-40m apart, kick to a target on the lead; add a jogging teammate so you dispose before contact.

### 3. Run and Carry
Carrying the ball out of defence breaks lines and draws opponents.
- Dribble-bounce through 4-5 cones at jogging pace, finish with a handball or short kick.

### 4. Spoiling and One-on-One Defending
When you can't mark it, punching the ball clear keeps it out of dangerous hands.
- One-handed punch through the line; then body the opponent before spoiling.

## Common Mistakes

- Standing too close to your opponent, removing your space to intercept.
- Kicking blind down the line into a contest instead of finding a teammate.
- Getting drawn up the ground and leaving your zone open behind you.
- Watching the ball instead of your opponent.

## Smart Conditioning (Over-35s)

- Proper 10-minute warm-up (jog, leg swings, strides) every session.
- Short, sharp repeat efforts (10-15s with full recovery) rather than long sprints.
- Twice-weekly calf raises and single-leg balance to protect Achilles and ankles.
- Treat any niggle seriously and rest a session rather than missing a month.

## What Good Looks Like

- You win or spoil the ball in your zone and your opponent rarely gets a clean mark.
- Your exit kicks find a teammate more often than not.
- You communicate, hold structure, and are still in position late in the game.
