# Back Pocket — AFL Masters Development Guide

## Role Summary

The Back Pocket is a defender who lines up deep in the defensive 50, usually next to the full-back, responsible for shutting down a small or medium forward while protecting the goal directly behind. You play close to your opponent, win the ball when it spills loose, and start your team's run out of defence with safe, controlled disposal. It is a role built on awareness, smart positioning, and discipline rather than raw speed or height. For an over-35 player, it rewards reading the play and being in the right spot.

## Positioning & Movement

- Stand goal-side and ball-side of your opponent, between them and the goals while still seeing the ball.
- Hold a deep position when the ball is up the other end; push up only as your team gains territory.
- Keep your head on a swivel: glance between the ball and your opponent every few seconds.
- When the ball is in dispute, step off your opponent to give yourself room to attack or punch it clear.

## Core Skills & Drills

### 1. Spoiling (One-Handed Punch)
Your safest play when you cannot win the ball cleanly.
- Partner kicks high; time a one-handed punch over their shoulder; add someone to gather the spoil.

### 2. Defensive Body Positioning
Block your opponent's run to the ball legally, without pace.
- Shadow drill: stay goal-side of a jogging partner as they change direction, contact light, feet active.

### 3. Short, Safe Kicking Under Pressure
Clears the danger zone without turning it over in front of goal.
- Kick to a target 20-30m away while a partner applies token chasing pressure, both feet.

### 4. Reading the Ball in Flight
Position early instead of reacting late.
- Partner kicks balls at varied heights; call "mark," "spoil," or "ground ball" early, then execute.

## Common Mistakes

- Ball-watching and losing track of your opponent, who leads into space behind you.
- Going for a risky mark when a simple spoil was safer in front of goal.
- Kicking across the face of goal instead of clearing wide or to a clear target.
- Standing flat-footed; keep your feet moving to react either direction.

## Smart Conditioning (Over-35s)

- Proper warm-up with leg swings, lunges, and build-up runs before jumping or sprinting.
- Basic calf, hamstring, and glute strength twice a week to protect against soft-tissue injuries.
- Train change-of-direction in short controlled bursts rather than flat-out sprints.
- Don't load up heavily the day before a game; treat niggles early.

## What Good Looks Like

- Your direct opponent has a quiet day with few marks and fewer goals.
- You repeatedly spoil or clear the ball safely under pressure without panicking.
- You start attacks with calm, accurate disposal that keeps the ball in your team's hands.
