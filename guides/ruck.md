# Ruck (Ruckman) — AFL Masters Development Guide

## Role Summary

The ruck is the tall player who contests stoppages, jumping to tap the ball at centre bounces, boundary throw-ins, and ball-ups. Your job is to win first use of the ball for your midfielders by hitting it to a teammate's advantage, not just hitting it anywhere. Around the ground you act as a mobile target up forward and a spare big body in defence. In over-35s footy, smart positioning and timing matter far more than raw spring, so play to craft, not athleticism.

## Positioning & Movement

- At centre bounces, set up a step back from the circle so you can run onto the contest rather than standing under it.
- Read the umpire's throw or bounce early and commit your jump late, timing the rise to meet the ball at its peak.
- After the tap, don't switch off. Follow up at ground level or push to a forward/defensive position.
- At boundary throw-ins, body up against your opponent legally to deny them a clean leap.

## Core Skills & Drills

### 1. Ruck Taps to Advantage
A controlled tap to a runner beats a big spoiling fist into nowhere.
- Teammate rolls/throws a ball up while a third calls a direction; tap softly to that side, 20 reps each.

### 2. Timing the Leap
Meeting the ball at the top of your jump wins the contest even against taller opponents.
- Partner bounces a ball; step in and jump to palm it down, focusing on a late takeoff.

### 3. Bodywork at Stoppages
Legal contact lets a slower ruck still win position.
- Practise shepherding/holding your spot before the ball arrives, then release into the contest.

### 4. Marking as a Target
Rucks are big leads up forward and reliable kick-out receivers.
- Lead hard at a kicker, present a strong two-handed mark, 15 leads at low intensity.

## Common Mistakes

- Jumping too early and being on the way down when the ball arrives.
- Tapping hard and aimlessly instead of placing it to a teammate.
- Standing still after the tap and watching.
- Over-committing to the leap every contest and gassing out by the third quarter.

## Smart Conditioning (Over-35s)

- Warm up calves, achilles, and hips before any jumping; cold tendons tear easily.
- Single-leg strength (calf raises, step-ups) once a week to protect knees and ankles on landings.
- Pace your leaping in matches; pick your contests rather than going up every bounce.
- Prioritise recovery: light mobility, hydration, and sleep do more than extra reps.

## What Good Looks Like

- More of your taps land with a teammate than with the opposition.
- You're still contesting and following up at the final stoppages.
- Your midfielders get clean, predictable first use from your hitouts.
