# Centre Half Back (CHB) — AFL Masters Development Guide

## Role Summary

The Centre Half Back is the defensive general, positioned in the middle of the half-back line and tasked with shutting down the opposition's Centre Half Forward, usually one of their tallest and most dangerous targets. You are the last line of organised defence before goal, so reading the play, intercepting long kicks, and rebounding the ball cleanly out of defence are your core jobs. This is a position built on positioning and decision-making more than raw pace, which suits experienced over-35 players. Think of yourself as the anchor: calm, vocal, and always between your opponent and the goal.

## Positioning & Movement

- Stay goal-side of your opponent by default, then peel off to attack the ball when it's in the air.
- Hold a position slightly in front when the ball is coming in long, giving you first crack at the intercept.
- Drop back into the "hole" in front of goal when the ball is on the far side, becoming a spare defender.
- Move early, not fast: take your first step as the kick leaves the boot.

## Core Skills & Drills

### 1. Intercept Marking
Winning the ball before it reaches their forward is the single most valuable thing a CHB does.
- Partner kicks high to a spot; start a few metres behind and time your run to take it at the highest point.

### 2. Spoiling
When you can't mark it, a clean punch to ground keeps the forward from a contested grab.
- One-handed punch through the back of the contest, hitting the ball not the body.

### 3. Kicking Under Pressure
Your rebound kick sets up the counter-attack.
- Kick to a leading target from a stationary then moving start; add a chasing teammate.

### 4. Defensive Bodywork
Using your body to hold position and block the lead protects the space behind you.
- Shadow a partner leading to a spot, staying side-on and goal-side without grabbing.

## Common Mistakes

- Ball-watching and losing your opponent, letting them sneak behind for an easy lead.
- Going for a spectacular mark you can't reach; punch it if in doubt.
- Kicking long and aimless under pressure instead of finding a short target.
- Drifting too far up the ground and leaving the space in front of goal unguarded.

## Smart Conditioning (Over-35s)

- Warm up calf, hamstring, and groin before any marking or sprinting; standing-then-sprinting is a common trigger.
- Single-leg strength (step-ups, calf raises, light squats) once a week to protect knees and ankles.
- Train repeat-effort with short jog-to-stride efforts rather than long runs.
- Practise landing on two feet from marks; ease off heavy aerial work if knees or back are sore.

## What Good Looks Like

- The ball rarely hits the ground cleanly in your area: you mark it or punch it clear.
- Your direct opponent has a quiet day, kept goal-side and starved of easy leads.
- Your rebound kicks hit a target and start attacks rather than just clearing the ball.
