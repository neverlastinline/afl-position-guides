# Centre — AFL Masters Development Guide

## Role Summary

The Centre is the engine room of the midfield, stationed in the middle of the ground at every centre bounce and responsible for linking defence to attack. You need to win or gather the ball from the contest, then deliver it cleanly to teammates running forward. It is a high-running role that demands good decision-making under pressure and the willingness to both attack the ball and defend when possession is lost. Smart positioning and clean disposal matter far more than raw speed.

## Positioning & Movement

- At the centre bounce, set up slightly behind the ball drop so you can read the ruck tap and move onto it.
- Work in arcs, not straight lines, so you're always offering a forward option.
- Spread quickly once the ball clears the square, then recover back to the middle for the next stoppage.
- Stay goal-side of your direct opponent when the opposition wins the ball.

## Core Skills & Drills

### 1. Clearance Handball Under Pressure
Quick hands get the ball out before tacklers arrive.
- Kneel and handball to a partner; progress to picking up a rolling ball and handballing in one motion.

### 2. Kicking to a Target on the Move
Centres rarely kick standing still.
- Jog along a line and kick to a partner 20-25m away, alternating feet.

### 3. Ground-Ball Pickup
Most centre clearances start with a loose ball at your feet.
- Roll a ball away and chase to scoop it cleanly, 10 reps each side.

### 4. Two-Way Running
You must run both ways, attacking and defending.
- Shuttle runs between cones 30m apart, 6-8 reps.

## Common Mistakes

- Standing flat at the bounce and reacting late instead of anticipating the tap.
- Over-handballing into traffic when a quick kick to space was safer.
- Ball-watching and losing your opponent on the turnover.
- Running only forward and leaving the defence exposed on the rebound.

## Smart Conditioning (Over-35s)

- Warm up hamstrings, calves and groin with light jogging and dynamic stretches before sprint work.
- Build running volume gradually; short intervals rather than long flat-out runs.
- Single-leg balance and calf-raise work to support ankles and knees against cutting and twisting.
- Rotate off the ball when fatigued; a tired centre late is when soft-tissue injuries happen.

## What Good Looks Like

- You consistently get clean hands or boot to the ball at stoppages and find a teammate.
- You're visibly running both ways, offering options and helping defend.
- Your disposal is dependable under pressure.
